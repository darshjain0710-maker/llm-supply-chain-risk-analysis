# LLM-Based Supply Chain Risk Analysis

## Overview

This project develops a Large Language Model (LLM)-based framework for identifying and classifying emerging supply-chain risks from unstructured textual data. The framework transforms customer reviews and logistics-related sentiment into structured risk indicators, enabling early detection of disruptions across critical supply-chain functions.

The project focuses on five major risk categories:

* Inventory Risk
* Delivery Risk
* Transportation Risk
* Supplier Risk
* Product Quality Risk

By leveraging prompt engineering and LLM-driven classification, the framework aims to improve supply-chain visibility and support proactive risk management decisions.

---

## Objectives

* Develop a structured supply-chain risk taxonomy.
* Extract risk signals from customer reviews and logistics text.
* Classify risks using Large Language Models.
* Evaluate prompting strategies for risk identification.
* Generate structured risk indicators from unstructured data.

---

## Methodology

### Data Collection

* Customer reviews
* Logistics feedback
* Supply-chain related textual records

### Data Preprocessing

* Text cleaning
* Duplicate removal
* Standardization
* Risk-category mapping

### Risk Taxonomy Design

The framework categorizes risks into:

| Category       | Description                                |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Inventory      | Stockouts, shortages, replenishment issues |
| Delivery       | Delayed or failed deliveries               |
| Transportation | Shipping and logistics disruptions         |
| Supplier       | Vendor reliability and sourcing issues     |
| Quality        | Product defects and quality concerns       |

### LLM-Based Classification

The model processes textual inputs and assigns risk labels using prompt-based classification techniques.
